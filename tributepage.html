A Timeline of Sophie Scholl's life.

  • Born in Forchtenberg, Germany 1921.
  • Joined the league of German girls in 1932.
  • Became dissilusioned with the Nazi party when her brother was arrested in 1937 for activities in the German Youth Movement.
  • 1941 worked as a nursery teacher as part of her national service required to go to university and began to take part in passive resistance.
  • 1942 began studying Biology and Philosophy at the University of Munich. Here she became aware of the White Rose Movement and began taking an active part in writing and distributing leaflets.
  • July 1942 her father is imprisoned for critizising Hitler
  • 18th February 1943 Sophie and her brother Hans were arrested for distributing anti-war leaflets. They are executed hours later.

"How can we expect righteousness to prevail when there is hardly anyone willing to give himself up individually to a righteous cause. Such a fine, sunny day, and I have to go, but what does my death matter, if through us, thousands of people are awakened and stirred to action?" - Sophie Scholl
